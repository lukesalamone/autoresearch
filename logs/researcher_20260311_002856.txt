Analysis:
Based on the results so far, I can see that:
1. The best MRR@10 achieved is 0.4519 (exp_001) with embed_dim=128, hidden_dim=128, 2 layers.
2. The model size is 8.33MB, which is over the 10MB limit, but it's using the pretrained init.
3. The factorized embeddings experiments (exp_002 to exp_016) show that smaller embed_dim and more layers generally lead to better MRR@10, but also higher model size.
4. The best factorized results so far are:
   - exp_010: MRR=0.4379, embed=64, hidden=192, 2 layers, 5.07MB
   - exp_008: MRR=0.4488, embed=48, hidden=192, 2 layers, 4.13MB
   - exp_007: MRR=0.4150, embed=32, hidden=192, 3 layers, 3.76MB
   - exp_005: MRR=0.4252, embed=64, hidden=128, 2 layers, 4.37MB

The exploration shows that:
- 2-layer models with larger hidden_dim (192) and smaller embed_dim (32 or 48) are promising
- 3-layer models with embed=32 and hidden=192 are also good
- The size budget is tight, with ~6.3MB being the limit for 2-layer models with hidden=256

I will focus on exploring:
1. 2-layer models with embed=32 and hidden=256 (which should be around 6.3MB)
2. 3-layer models with embed=32 and hidden=256 (should be around 6.3MB)
3. 2-layer models with embed=48 and hidden=256 (should be around 6.3MB)
4. 2-layer models with embed=64 and hidden=256 (should be around 6.3MB)
5. 3-layer models with embed=48 and hidden=256 (should be around 6.3MB)
These are all around the 6.3MB boundary, which is a good balance between size and performance.
