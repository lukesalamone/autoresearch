Analysis:
The best MRR@10 so far is 0.4681 (exp_024, exp_028), achieved with a 2-layer model (embed=48, hidden=192, heads=3, intermediate=384) using mean pooling and lr=0.0001. The model size was 4.13MB. 

There's been some exploration of larger hidden_dim (256) and embed_dim (64), but they're hitting the 10MB limit. The current best architecture is quite stable, so I'll focus on fine-tuning the learning rate, pooling strategy, and potentially adding a projection head to improve performance without increasing model size.

The last few runs have shown that:
- Mean pooling is generally better than weighted_mean (exp_024 vs 030, 041)
- lr=0.0001 is better than lr=0.0002 or 0.00005 (exp_024 vs 029, 034, 039)
- 2-layer models are more stable than 3-layer (exp_033 vs 024, 028)
- Smaller embed_dim (32) works well with hidden=192 (exp_036 vs 024)

I'll now try a few variations:
1. A 2-layer model with embed=32, hidden=192, lr=0.00015 (a bit higher than 0.0001)
2. A 2-layer model with embed=32, hidden=192, lr=0.00005 (lower learning rate)
3. A 2-layer model with embed=32, hidden=192, pooling=weighted_mean (to see if it's better than mean)
4. A 2-layer model with embed=48, hidden=192, lr=0.0001, pooling=weighted_mean (to see if this improves on best mean)
5. A 2-layer model with embed=32, hidden=192, lr=0.0001, pooling=weighted_mean, intermediate=512 (larger FFN)
6. A 2-layer model with embed=48, hidden=192, lr=0.0001, pooling=weighted_mean, intermediate=512 (larger FFN)
7. A 2-layer model with embed=32, hidden=192, lr=0.0001, pooling=mean, intermediate=512 (larger FFN)
